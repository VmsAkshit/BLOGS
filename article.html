How I reverse-engineered the Class 10 Math Board Paper.

Akshit Published April 25, 2026 • 5 min read

Most students approach the Class 10 Board exams completely wrong. They open the NCERT textbook to page one and try to solve every single question, front to back. By February, they are burned out, exhausted, and terrified of the geometry section.

I didn't have time for that. I wanted to build StudoAI while studying, so I needed a hack. I decided to stop studying the book, and start studying the exam.

The 80/20 Rule in Mathematics

The Pareto Principle states that 80% of results come from 20% of the effort. In the context of CBSE board exams, this means that a very small pool of concepts generates the vast majority of the high-mark questions.

"You are not graded on how many hours you sat at your desk. You are graded on your ability to execute specific mathematical proofs under pressure."

To prove this, I printed out the last 10 years of Mathematics Standard question papers. I went through them with three different colored highlighters:

  • Red: Questions that appeared almost identically every single year (e.g., Prove that √3 is irrational).
  • Yellow: Variations of the same core formula (e.g., Section formula in Coordinate Geometry).
  • Green: Wildcard questions that rarely repeat.

The Golden Formulas

Once I finished highlighting, the pattern was shockingly obvious. You don't need to memorize 50 formulas. You need to deeply understand about 8 core mechanics, and learn how examiners try to trick you by twisting the wording.

For example, in Quadratic Equations, the discriminant ($b^2 - 4ac$) dictates everything. If you master exactly how the nature of roots connects to word problems, you automatically secure 5-7 marks on every paper.

How to apply this today

Stop doing random exercises. Go to the StudoAI Arena, set the filter to "Previous Year Core", and grind the Red and Yellow concepts until you can solve them in your sleep. Once your foundation is bulletproof, then you can worry about the wildcard questions.
